Handout 6: surveillance, media & fake-news

6.1 surveillance

facts

  • Surveillance is the monitoring of behavior, activities or information for the purpose of influencing, managing or directing.
  • It can be used by the government (as prevention of crime, protection, investigating a crime, …)
  • It can be used by criminal organizations (to plan and commit a crime)
  • Surveillance can be viewed as violation of privacy. Liberal democracies often have laws against surveillance,
    authoritarian governments don't have any laws that restrict surveillance.
  • Most of surveillance comes from laptops and mobile phones.

advantages and disadvantages

advantages disadvantages
Surveillance can help with investigating a crime Everyone can find out who you are, where you are and other personal things
Protection (for example cameras at home) You could be watched at home via phone or laptop camera
It can relieve the police (for example with speed cameras) Personal data can be used by criminal organizations
You feel observed by all the cameras in public
You can be manipulated and controlled

controversial issues

  • Is surveillance a good method to prevent crimes?
  • Surveillance - curse or blessing for the people?
  • How much surveillance is good for a state and how much is too much?
  • Should the RKI be allowed to take location data from German phones?

new developments

In Germany the RKI demands location data from all residents to see how many people are on the street and control how well the coronavirus restrictions are adhered to.

6.2 media

forms of media

  • classical forms of media:
    • radio
    • television
    • newspaper
  • modern forms of media (evolved and possible through the internet):
    • social media
    • videos (eg. from YouTube)
    • e-mail
    • blogs
    • podcasts
    • video games

influence of media on the society

  • media influences the behavior of almost all people
  • many people believe everything they hear, read or see in the media
  • media can manipulate, influence, persuade and pressurize society so much, that it can control the world at times in positive and negative ways
  • users of social media like e.g. Instagram feel the social pressure to present themselves in an extremely positive light, people show off, this leads to often unrealistic ideas of how happy/ successful / active other peple are.
  • Influencers are very often more influential than traditional media who put a lot of effort into researching their articles -> fake news

controversial issues

  • Does media have more control over humans than governments?
  • Is media the extra-parliamentary opposition?
  • Does media affect us too much in our daily life?

new developments

since the start of the coronavirus-regulations, the media got even more important than before:

  • home office: online conferences
  • more people watch the news
  • more contact via social media

6.3 fake-news

definition

Fake News is a form of news with no basis in fact, but being presented as accurate with the goal to manipulate the reader.

history

  • In the 13th century BC, Rameses the Great spread lies and propaganda portraying the Battle of Kadesh as a stunning victory for the Egyptians. The treaty reveals however, that the battle was a stalemate
  • In the Medieval time fake news stories were made up to harm the business of many Jews. A lot of people had depts with Jews, so they were happy to spread them. Many Jews were getting attacked and burned at the stake.
  • In the 20th century fake-news were mostly used in form of propaganda against other countries or ethnicities from governments.
  • In the 21st century the term “fake-news” made a comeback through the 2016 Presidential election. Trump claimed a lot of times, that the press spread fake news about him.

controversial issues

  • What possibilities do we have to stop the spread of fake-news?
  • Should spreading fake-news be punishable?

new developments

  • Fake news is generally a current issue, which grew with modern forms of media, because the speed at which information are spread is so much higher than with conventional media
  • Especially politicians like Trump do spread fake news
  • Now, with COVID-19, more fake-news is spread in the world (alleged cures)
